Timeline of Susan B. Anthony's Life

  • 1820 - Born, Adams, Mass.
  • 1849 - Joined the Daughters of Temperance in Canajoharie, N.Y. By March 1849, had become Presiding Sister of the Montgomery Union, No. 29, of the Daughters of Temperance in Canajoharie, a position she also held after moving to Rochester, N.Y., and joining that city's union in mid-1849
  • 1851 - Met Elizabeth Cady Stanton; enlisted by her in woman's rights cause
  • 1854 - Organized and participated in a canvass to obtain signatures on petitions demanding woman suffrage and improvement of the Married Woman's Property Law in New York
  • 1870 - Founded, with Elizabeth Cady Stanton, the National Woman Suffrage Association to agitate for a 16th Amendment that would outlaw disfranchisement on account of sex; provided leadership of NWSA until its merger in 1890 with the American Woman Suffrage Association
  • 1906 -Elizabeth Cady Stanton published the controversial The Woman's Bible (New York: European Publishing Co. 2 vols.)
  • 1906 - Died, Rochester, N.Y.
